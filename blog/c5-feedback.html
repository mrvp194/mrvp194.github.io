Pairing & Feedback

11/9/2014

Pairing has been a very interesting experience to undertake over the past couple weeks. Initially, I was pretty nervous to pair since I'm normally a lone ranger when it comes to tackling a challenge. After my first pairing session, however, I realized how powerful it could be. It really helped reduce errors I might've made in my code and sped up my efficiency quite a bit. At times it's hard to keep the Navigator/Driving roles. When we hit a roadblock because it becomes an almost frantic search to figure out the next step. I would like to improve on this in the future.

Similar to pairing, I felt fairly nervous looking at my first piece of feedback. Luckily, it was all pretty positive. So far I've recieved pretty good reviews so I don't have much to complain about. One thing I have noticed is that I usually don't recieve much actionable feedback. Sometimes it's hard pinpoint what could really be improved upon in a session and there are times I can tell the person just made something up. I find I have similar struggles when writing feedback for another. I actually just finished a program where feedback was very much incorporated into the environment and considered to be crucial to our development, so you'd think I'd be a bit more used to it. Unfortunately, I still do not enjoy giving feedback and I fear I never will. It's hard to be completely honest with someone, even with anonymoty. This is something I would definetely like to improve upon going foward.
